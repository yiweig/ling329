Emory University is a private research university in metropolitan Atlanta, located in the Druid Hills section of unincorporated DeKalb County, Georgia, United States.[16] The university was founded as Emory College in 1836 in Oxford, Georgia by the Methodist Episcopal Church and was named in honor of Methodist bishop John Emory.[17] In 1915, the college relocated to metropolitan Atlanta and was rechartered as Emory University.[18] The university is the second-oldest private institution of higher education in Georgia and among the fifty oldest private universities in the United States.[19]

Emory University has nine academic divisions: Emory College of Arts and Sciences, Oxford College, Goizueta Business School, Laney Graduate School, School of Law, School of Medicine, Nell Hodgson Woodruff School of Nursing, Rollins School of Public Health, and the Candler School of Theology.[20] Emory University, the Georgia Institute of Technology, and Peking University in Beijing, China jointly administer the Wallace H. Coulter Department of Biomedical Engineering.[21][22] The university operates the Confucius Institute in Atlanta in partnership with Nanjing University.[23][24] Emory has a growing faculty research partnership with the Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST).[25][26][27] Emory University students come from all 50 states, 6 territories of the United States, and over 100 foreign countries.[28]

Emory Healthcare is the largest healthcare system in the state of Georgia[29] and comprises seven major hospitals, including the internationally renowned Emory University Hospital and Emory University Hospital Midtown.[30] The university operates the Winship Cancer Institute, Yerkes National Primate Research Center, and many disease and vaccine research centers.[31][32][33] Emory university is one of four institutions involved in the NIAID's Tuberculosis Research Units Program[34] and is the leading coordinator of the U.S. Health Department's National Ebola Training and Education Center.[35] The International Association of National Public Health Institutes is headquartered at the university[36] and the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention and the American Cancer Society are national affiliate institutions located adjacent to the campus.[37] The university is partnered with the Carter Center.[38]

Emory University is 18th among the list of colleges and universities in the United States by endowment,[39] 21st among universities in the world by endowment,[40] and 21st in U.S. News & World Report's 2016 National Universities Rankings.[41] Emory University has a Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education status of RU/VH: "very high research activity" and is cited for high scientific performance and citation impact in the CWTS Leiden Ranking.[42] Emory University research is funded primarily by federal government agencies, namely the National Institutes of Health (NIH).[43] In 1995 Emory University was elected to the Association of American Universities, an association of the 62 leading research universities in the United States & Canada.[11]
